ABOUT ME

Hello there!

Hey! I’m Rhinorenz Esguerra from Taytay, Rizal, Philippines. I’m currently studying Information Technology at ICCT (Institute of Creative Computer Technology), and I also serve in the Social Communications Ministry at the Minor Basilica and Parish of St. John the Baptist in Taytay.

I’ve been passionate about photography for as long as I can remember. It all started when I was 15, during a beach trip with my friends. One of them had a camera and was taking random shots of us laughing, smiling, building sandcastles. I got curious, looked at the photos, and realized how powerful it was to capture moments like that. I told him to stop taking pics for a while and just enjoy the trip with us. Instead, he handed me his camera and said I could try it out or just keep it in the room. I didn’t know anything about cameras back then, but I gave it a shot anyway and I instantly got hooked. Taking photos of my friends having fun made me realize how special it is to preserve memories like that.

Fast forward to when I turned 19, I hit a point where I felt kind of lost about what I wanted in life. My mom had always encouraged me to be a sacristan back in high school, but I always refused. One day, though, she suggested something different joining the SOCOM (Social Communications Ministry) at our parish. I agreed, not really expecting much, but that decision changed a lot for me. They taught me how to use a camera, and as the group grew, more experienced photographers joined. I started learning from them, asking questions, and picking up tips. Eventually, I found myself falling even deeper in love with photography.

"Always be kind to others, because you never really know what they’re going through."
